# CATEGORY 1: Dental Anatomy — Tooth Surfaces & Directional Terms

## Tooth Surfaces (5 per tooth)
1. Mesial (M) — surface closest to the midline of the face
2. Distal (D) — surface away from the midline
3. Buccal (B) — surface facing the cheek (posterior teeth)
4. Labial (La) — surface facing the lips (anterior teeth)
5. Facial (F) — umbrella term for buccal + labial
6. Lingual (Li) — surface facing the tongue (mandibular teeth)
7. Palatal (P) — surface facing the palate (maxillary teeth)
8. Occlusal (O) — chewing surface (premolars and molars)
9. Incisal (I) — biting edge (incisors and canines)
10. Proximal — mesial or distal surfaces collectively
11. Interproximal — space between adjacent teeth

## Combined Surface Terms
12. Mesio-occlusal (MO)
13. Disto-occlusal (DO)
14. Mesio-occluso-distal (MOD)
15. Mesio-buccal (MB)
16. Disto-buccal (DB)
17. Mesio-lingual (ML)
18. Disto-lingual (DL)
19. Bucco-lingual
20. Facio-lingual

## Tooth Anatomy
21. Crown — visible portion above gumline
22. Root — portion embedded in bone
23. Enamel — hard outer covering of crown
24. Dentin — layer beneath enamel
25. Cementum — thin covering of root surface
26. Pulp — soft tissue containing nerves and blood vessels
27. Pulp chamber — central space in the crown
28. Root canal — channel within the root
29. Apex / apices — tip of the root
30. Apical foramen — opening at the root apex
31. Cemento-enamel junction (CEJ) — line where enamel meets cementum
32. Furcation — area where roots divide in multi-rooted teeth
33. Cusp — pointed elevation on occlusal surface
34. Fossa — depression or concavity on tooth surface
35. Central fossa — main depression on occlusal surface
36. Pit — small depression at junction of developmental grooves
37. Fissure — narrow groove in enamel
38. Ridge — linear elevation on tooth surface
39. Marginal ridge — raised border on mesial/distal edges
40. Triangular ridge — ridge from cusp tip to center of occlusal surface
41. Oblique ridge — diagonal ridge on maxillary molars
42. Transverse ridge — union of buccal and lingual triangular ridges
43. Cingulum — convex area on lingual surface of anterior teeth
44. Mamelons — rounded bumps on incisal edges of newly erupted incisors

## Oral Anatomy
45. Maxilla — upper jaw
46. Mandible — lower jaw
47. Maxillary — pertaining to upper jaw
48. Mandibular — pertaining to lower jaw
49. Alveolar bone — bone surrounding and supporting teeth
50. Alveolar ridge — bony ridge containing tooth sockets
51. Alveolus — tooth socket
52. Gingiva — gum tissue
53. Free gingiva — unattached gingival margin
54. Attached gingiva — gingiva firmly bound to bone
55. Gingival sulcus — groove between tooth and free gingiva
56. Gingival margin — edge of the gum tissue
57. Interdental papilla — gingival tissue between adjacent teeth
58. Mucogingival junction — boundary between attached gingiva and alveolar mucosa
59. Palate — roof of the mouth
60. Hard palate — bony anterior portion of palate
61. Soft palate — muscular posterior portion of palate
62. Uvula — tissue hanging from the soft palate
63. Vestibule — space between lips/cheeks and teeth
64. Floor of the mouth
65. Frenulum (labial/lingual) — tissue fold connecting lip or tongue to gingiva
66. Tongue (dorsum, ventral, lateral)
67. Retromolar area — area behind the last molar
68. Tuberosity — rounded bony prominence behind maxillary molars
69. Temporomandibular joint (TMJ)
70. Condyle — rounded bony projection of the mandible at TMJ
71. Ramus — vertical portion of the mandible
72. Mental foramen — opening on the mandible for nerve passage
73. Inferior alveolar nerve
74. Lingual nerve
75. Buccal nerve
